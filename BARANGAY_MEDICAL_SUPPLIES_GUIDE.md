# Barangay Medical Supplies Inventory System
## Complete Process Guide for Philippine Barangay Health Centers

## Overview

This Medical Supplies Inventory Management System is specifically designed for Philippine Barangay Health Centers (BHCs) following Department of Health (DOH) guidelines and local government unit (LGU) protocols.

## Supply Chain Process in Philippine Barangay Health Centers

### Phase 1: **Planning & Requisition**

#### 1.1 Inventory Assessment
- **Frequency**: Weekly or Bi-weekly
- **Process**: 
  - BHW (Barangay Health Worker) or Midwife conducts physical count
  - Compare actual stock vs. recorded stock
  - Identify items below reorder level
  - Check for expired or near-expiry items

#### 1.2 Preparation of Request
- **Documents Required**:
  - RIS (Requisition and Issue Slip)
  - Summary of Inventory Status
  - Justification for quantities needed
  
- **Approval Levels**:
  1. Barangay Health Worker prepares the list
  2. Barangay Captain reviews and signs
  3. Municipal/City Health Officer approves

### Phase 2: **Procurement/Receiving**

#### 2.1 Sources of Medical Supplies

**A. Department of Health (DOH)**
- Free distribution through provincial/regional DOH offices
- Usually includes:
  - Vaccines (EPI Program)
  - Family Planning commodities
  - Prenatal vitamins
  - Micronutrient supplements
  - Basic medicines

**B. Local Government Unit (LGU)**
- Municipal/City Health Office allocation
- Funded by local health budget
- Regular quarterly or monthly distribution

**C. PhilHealth**
- Primary Care Benefit (PCB) supplies
- Konsulta package items
- Preventive care commodities

**D. Barangay Procurement**
- Using Barangay Health Fund
- For urgent or specific needs
- Through local suppliers

**E. Donations**
- NGOs and private organizations
- Corporate social responsibility programs
- International aid organizations

#### 2.2 Receiving Process

**Required Documents**:
- Delivery Receipt (DR) or Sales Invoice (SI)
- Purchase Order (PO) reference (if applicable)
- Inspection and Acceptance Report (IAR)

**Receiving Steps**:
1. **Physical Inspection**
   - Check quantity against DR/SI
   - Verify item descriptions and specifications
   - Check for damages or defects
   - Verify expiry dates (minimum 6 months validity)

2. **Documentation**
   - Record in Supplies Ledger
   - Update inventory cards
   - File copies of DR/SI
   - Note batch numbers and expiry dates

3. **Storage**
   - Arrange by category
   - Apply FEFO (First Expired, First Out)
   - Proper labeling
   - Maintain cold chain (if applicable)

4. **Acknowledgment**
   - Receiver signs DR/SI
   - Stamp "Received" with date
   - Designate BHW or authorized personnel name

### Phase 3: **Storage Management**

#### 3.1 Storage Principles

**Temperature Control**:
- Room temperature items: 15-30°C
- Refrigerated items: 2-8°C (vaccines)
- Away from direct sunlight

**Organization**:
- Separate by category
- Arrange by expiry date
- Label shelves clearly
- Maintain cleanliness

**Security**:
- Locked storage area
- Limited access (authorized personnel only)
- Inventory cards per item
- Daily temperature monitoring (for cold chain)

#### 3.2 Stock Monitoring

**Daily Tasks**:
- Check temperature logs
- Visual inspection for damages
- Update manual/digital logs

**Weekly Tasks**:
- Physical count verification
- Identify slow-moving items
- Check near-expiry items

**Monthly Tasks**:
- Full inventory count
- Reconciliation report
- Stock status report to Barangay Council

### Phase 4: **Dispensing/Issuance**

#### 4.1 Authorized Purposes

Medical supplies can only be issued for:
- **Patient Treatment**: Direct patient care
- **Health Programs**: 
  - Prenatal and maternal care
  - Child immunization (EPI)
  - Family planning services
  - TB-DOTS program
  - Senior citizens health services
- **Medical Missions**: Barangay or municipal health events
- **Emergency Response**: Disasters, accidents, outbreaks
- **School Health Programs**: Deworming, dental missions

#### 4.2 Issuance Process

**Required Documentation**:
- Issue Slip (IS) or Dispensing Record
- Patient registry (if applicable)
- Program monitoring form

**Procedure**:
1. Verify request from authorized personnel
2. Check available stock
3. Record issuance details:
   - Date and time
   - Item name and quantity
   - Issued to (name/program)
   - Purpose
   - Issued by (BHW name and signature)
   - Received by (recipient signature)

4. Update inventory records
5. File documentation

#### 4.3 Documentation

**Patient-Level Issuance**:
- Record in Patient Chart
- Note in Individual Treatment Record (ITR)

**Program-Level Issuance**:
- Record in Program Monitoring Forms
  - Prenatal Tracking Form
  - Immunization Monitoring Sheet
  - Family Planning Client Record

### Phase 5: **Monitoring & Reporting**

#### 5.1 Regular Reports

**Weekly Report** (Internal):
- Fast-moving items
- Low stock alerts
- Expired items for disposal

**Monthly Report** (to Municipal Health Office):
- Beginning balance
- Items received
- Items issued/consumed
- Ending balance
- Summary of programs served

**Quarterly Report** (to DOH/LGU):
- Comprehensive inventory status
- Utilization rate per program
- Requests for replenishment
- Issues and concerns

#### 5.2 Stock Status Indicators

**In Stock**: Quantity above reorder level (Green)
**Low Stock**: Quantity at or below reorder level (Yellow/Orange)
**Out of Stock**: Zero quantity (Red - Immediate Action)

**Reorder Level Formula**:
```
Reorder Level = (Average Daily Consumption × Lead Time) + Safety Stock
```

Example:
- Average daily use of alcohol: 2 bottles
- Lead time for replenishment: 7 days
- Safety stock: 5 bottles
- Reorder Level = (2 × 7) + 5 = 19 bottles

### Phase 6: **Disposal & Write-Off**

#### 6.1 Grounds for Disposal

- Expired items (beyond expiry date)
- Damaged/contaminated supplies
- Recalled products (DOH advisory)
- Obsolete items

#### 6.2 Disposal Process

**Documentation**:
- Prepare Inventory Disposal Slip
- List items with reasons
- Note quantity and estimated value

**Approval**:
1. Barangay Health Worker documents
2. Barangay Captain endorses
3. Municipal Health Officer approves
4. Municipal Accountant for write-off

**Method**:
- Follow DOH Healthcare Waste Management guidelines
- Segregate per waste category
- Use authorized waste disposal service
- Document with Certificate of Disposal

## Best Practices for Barangay Health Centers

### Inventory Management
1. **Conduct regular physical counts** - At least monthly
2. **Apply FEFO principle** - First Expired, First Out
3. **Maintain cleanliness** - Clean storage area weekly
4. **Update records immediately** - Don't delay documentation
5. **Segregate by category** - Easy location and monitoring

### Documentation
1. **Keep complete files** - All DR/SI, Issue Slips
2. **Backup digital records** - Regular system backups
3. **Standardize forms** - Use templates consistently
4. **File chronologically** - Easy retrieval
5. **Retain for required period** - Minimum 3 years

### Communication
1. **Coordinate with MHO** - Regular updates
2. **Report issues promptly** - Stock-outs, damages
3. **Submit timely reports** - Meet deadlines
4. **Share with Barangay Council** - Transparency

### Quality Assurance
1. **Check expiry dates** - During receiving and weekly
2. **Inspect packaging** - No tears or damages
3. **Proper storage** - Right temperature and location
4. **Control access** - Authorized personnel only
5. **Monitor usage patterns** - Identify wastage

## Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue 1: Stock-Out of Essential Items
**Prevention**:
- Set appropriate reorder levels
- Monitor consumption trends
- Maintain safety stock
- Coordinate early with MHO

**Solution**:
- Emergency request to MHO
- Borrow from nearby BHC (with proper documentation)
- Use Barangay funds for urgent procurement

### Issue 2: Expired Items
**Prevention**:
- Apply FEFO strictly
- Regular expiry date monitoring
- Adjust order quantities
- Coordinate with programs

**Solution**:
- Proper disposal process
- Document for write-off
- Review forecasting methods

### Issue 3: Discrepancies in Count
**Prevention**:
- Accurate recording at each transaction
- Regular reconciliation
- Secure storage

**Solution**:
- Conduct physical count
- Review documentation
- Investigate cause
- Implement controls

## Integration Points

### Database Requirements
You'll need to create:
1. `medical_supplies` table
2. `supply_receipts` table (incoming)
3. `supply_issuances` table (outgoing)
4. `supply_categories` table (optional)

### Controller Methods Needed
- `index()` - List all supplies
- `store()` - Add new supply item
- `receiveSupply()` - Record incoming supplies
- `issueSupply()` - Dispense supplies
- `updateStock()` - Adjust inventory
- `generateReport()` - Export reports

### Routes
```php
Route::prefix('medical-supplies')->group(function () {
    Route::get('/', [MedicalSupplyController::class, 'index'])->name('supplies.index');
    Route::post('/store', [MedicalSupplyController::class, 'store'])->name('supplies.store');
    Route::post('/receive', [MedicalSupplyController::class, 'receive'])->name('supplies.receive');
    Route::post('/issue', [MedicalSupplyController::class, 'issue'])->name('supplies.issue');
});
```
